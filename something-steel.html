ABOUT THE BAND

    Something Steel has been in existence for 3 years, but the nucleus of the band was together under another name for about 7 years previously.

    They have perform regularly around the region at both private and public events. They have played at Utica Squarein Tulsa for The Summer's Fifth Night concert series for may years, including last year when we drew an overflow crowd of about 2000 people. They have performed at many music festivals in the region including Mayfest, Greenwood Jazz Festival, and Rooster Days in Broken Arrow which always draw big crowds. They have performed at Drillers Stadium (Tulsa AA baseball franchise) for their annual Jimmy Buffett night promotion for the last 6 years. They have also play many fund-raising events and private parties for invididuals, country clubs, and corporations.

ABOUT THEIR MUSIC

    Something Steel plays feel-good sounds from the islands and specializes in the music of Jimmy Buffett. We play a combination of instrumental music and vocal music which appeals to a wide range of ages. The instrumentation of the band is: two steel drum players, vocals, electric guitar, bass guitar, and drums.

    At a typical Something Steel public conert, you will see small kids dancing, seniors tapping their feet, and lots of families.

    Something Steel is a favorite of the Parrot Head Clubs (Jimmy Buffett fan clubs) around the area.

ABOUT THE MUSICIANS

    All the band members have an extensive background in music including music degrees.

    Steel Drum: Chris Harris & Steve Craft

      Steel drum players, Chris Harris and Steve Craft, are also both directed at Owasso (formally Broken Arrow) and Bartlesville, respectively. Harris received his music education degree from The University of Oklahoma. His wife, Marti Harris, also sings vocals for the band and has performed professionally for several years. Craft also ahs played with the Tulsa Philharmonic for many years and received his traning at Louisville University.

    Guitar: David John

      Guitarist, David John, is a full-time musician and has performed for many top artists in the Tulsa area. He received a degree in Jazz Performance from the University of Northern Florida. He also operates a recording and production studio, Alpha & Omega Productions.

    Drums: Russell Grant

      Drummer, Russell Grant, is a full-time musician and has performed for many top artists in the Tulsa area. He received his training from The University of Tulsa.

    Bass & Vocalist

      Bass player and vocalist, Greg Spears, received his music performance degree from the University of Tulsa. He performs regularly with ensembles and symphonies in Northeastern Oklahoma.
